Managerial Roles (Henry Mintzberg)
[cite_start]There are three main categories of roles:   

[cite_start]

Interpersonal: Figurehead, leader, liaison.   

[cite_start]

Definition: Interacting with teams, customers, and stakeholders; influencing and motivating people.   

[cite_start]

Informational: Monitor, spokesperson.   

Definition: Acting as information hubs; they gather, share, and analyze data. [cite_start]This is crucial for decision-making and organizational alignment.   

[cite_start]

Decisional: Entrepreneur, negotiator, disturbance handler, resource allocator.   

[cite_start]

Definition: Making important decisions in resource allocation, problem-solving, and negotiating for the organization's benefit.   

[cite_start]

Note: This role usually belongs to top-line managers.   

Managerial Skills (Katz)
1. Conceptual Skills

Definition: Capacity to understand complex systems and abstract thinking. [cite_start]Involves strategic thinking and seeing the "big picture".   

[cite_start]

Relevance: Most important for top managers.   

2. Human Skills

[cite_start]

Definition: Ability to interact well, collaborate, motivate, and communicate.   

[cite_start]

Relevance: Important for all levels.   

3. Technical Skills

Definition: Proficiency in a certain task or tool. [cite_start]Involves specialized/job-related knowledge and tools.   

[cite_start]

Relevance: These skills reside more in baseline/non-managerial staff and first-line/bottom-line managers.
